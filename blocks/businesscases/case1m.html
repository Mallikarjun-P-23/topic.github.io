Waste Movement Management

The waste collected from Gandhi Nagar is transported using Dijkstra's Algorithm, where the area is represented as a graph with nodes for waste bins, collection points, and processing facilities . The algorithm calculates the shortest or most efficient routes based on factors like distance or time, ensuring optimal transport of waste types to their respective destinations .

Water Management

Consider for the same area we are required to supply water to that are ,to supply water we apply Breadth-First Search (BFS). It is used to traverse the network graph systematically, where nodes represent reservoirs, pipelines, or demand points, and edges represent water connections. BFS ensures water reaches all nodes by finding the shortest or most efficient paths in terms of connections. This is especially useful for detecting connectivity issues, identifying flow paths, or ensuring water supply reaches all demand points in a network.

Managing Huge amount of records

For managing records, Binary Search String Search is applied to efficiently search and retrieve records . When a query string is provided, BSSS uses binary search to compare the query with the middle record, narrowing the search range by half with each step. This process ensures fast and accurate retrieval of records in large datasets, minimizing search time while maintaining precision.
